## Weather & Stock Index Correlation

Daily historical weather for the **same seven cities** as `exchange_city` in `s4c_stock_indices` is stored in `s4c_meteo_historic` (from the shipped CSV, extended on schedule by `update_meteo_historic_csv.py` via Open-Meteo). Together with `s4c_stock_indices`, you can explore whether temperature, precipitation, or sunshine hours correlate with index performance.

**Weather fields:** `temperature_2m_mean` `temperature_2m_max` `precipitation_sum` `wind_speed_10m_max` `sunshine_duration` `weather_code`  
**Link key:** `city` in `s4c_meteo_historic` matches `exchange_city` in `s4c_stock_indices`

**Why `inputlookup meteo_historic` in joins:** A `join` subsearch is limited to 10,000 results by default. The meteo file has more daily rows than that, so a subsearch on `index=s4c_meteo_historic` can be truncated and the join shows empty weather. `inputlookup` uses the app lookup (same data as the index) without that cap. The app also ships a higher `subsearch` `maxout` in `limits.conf` for other joins.

### Explore the weather data

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_meteo_historic
| stats count min(_time) as oldest max(_time) as newest by city exchange
| eval oldest=strftime(oldest,"%Y-%m-%d"), newest=strftime(newest,"%Y-%m-%d")
| sort city`}></SplunkSearch>

---

### Join stocks + weather on date and exchange city

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stock_indices
| eval date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"))
| join type=left date exchange_city [
    | inputlookup meteo_historic
    | rename city as exchange_city
    | fields date exchange_city temperature_2m_mean precipitation_sum sunshine_duration
]
| table date exchange_city close temperature_2m_mean precipitation_sum sunshine_duration
| sort -date`}></SplunkSearch>

### DAX close vs Frankfurt temperature (monthly average)

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stock_indices index_name=DAX
| eval month=strftime(_time,"%Y-%m"), date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"))
| join type=left date [
    search index=s4c_meteo_historic city=Frankfurt
    | eval date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"))
    | fields date temperature_2m_mean sunshine_duration
]
| stats avg(close) as avg_close avg(temperature_2m_mean) as avg_temp by month
| sort month`}></SplunkSearch>

### Rainy days vs trading volume — London / FTSE 100

Do traders trade less on rainy London days?

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stock_indices index_name="FTSE 100"
| eval date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"))
| join type=left date [
    search index=s4c_meteo_historic city=London
    | eval date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"))
    | fields date precipitation_sum
]
| eval rain_day=if(precipitation_sum>5,"rainy","dry")
| stats avg(volume) as avg_volume by rain_day`}></SplunkSearch>

### Sunshine hours vs daily price change — all exchanges

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stock_indices
| eval date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d")), daily_pct=round(((close-open)/open)*100,2)
| join type=left date exchange_city [
    | inputlookup meteo_historic
    | rename city as exchange_city
    | fields date exchange_city sunshine_duration temperature_2m_mean
]
| eval sunshine_h=round(sunshine_duration/3600,1)
| stats avg(daily_pct) as avg_change avg(sunshine_h) as avg_sunshine by exchange_city
| sort -avg_change`}></SplunkSearch>

### Extreme weather days — what happened to the index?

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_meteo_historic
| where wind_speed_10m_max > 60 OR precipitation_sum > 40
| eval date=strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"), weather_event=case(
    wind_speed_10m_max>60,"storm",
    precipitation_sum>40,"heavy rain",
    1=1,"other")
| rename city as exchange_city
| join type=left date exchange_city [
   search index=s4c_stock_indices
   | eval date=coalesce(date, strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d")), daily_pct=round(((close-open)/open)*100,2)
   | fields date exchange_city close daily_pct volume
]
| table date city exchange_city weather_event wind_speed_10m_max precipitation_sum close daily_pct
| sort -date`}></SplunkSearch>
