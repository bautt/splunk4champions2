## Stock Index Metrics — Convert Events to Metrics

The `s4c_stocks` index contains 10 years of daily OHLCV data for 10 global indexes as **events**.  
In this exercise you will use `mcollect` to push that data into `s4c_student_metrics` as proper metrics.

**Fields in `s4c_stocks`:** `symbol` `index_name` `open` `high` `low` `close` `volume`  
**Available indexes:** DAX, S&P 500, NASDAQ, Dow Jones, FTSE 100, CAC 40, Euro Stoxx 50, Nikkei 225, Hang Seng, SMI

---

### Step 1: Explore the raw event data

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stocks
| stats count min(_time) as oldest max(_time) as newest by index_name symbol
| eval oldest=strftime(oldest,"%Y-%m-%d"), newest=strftime(newest,"%Y-%m-%d")
| sort index_name`}></SplunkSearch>

---

### Step 2: Preview what will become metrics

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stocks
| table _time symbol index_name open high low close volume
| sort -_time | head 20`}></SplunkSearch>

---

### Step 3: Convert to metrics with mcollect

The search below reads all stock events and writes them into `s4c_student_metrics`.  
The `prefix=stocks.` argument makes fields appear as `stocks.open`, `stocks.close` etc. in the Analytics Workspace.  
`index_name` and `symbol` become **dimensions**.

Remove the backtick comments from the `mcollect` line to actually write the data — **run once only**.

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`index=s4c_stocks
| fields _time symbol index_name open high low close volume
\`\`\`| mcollect index=s4c_student_metrics prefix=stocks.\`\`\``}></SplunkSearch>

> The full command when ready:  
> `| mcollect index=s4c_student_metrics prefix=stocks.`

---

### Step 4: Verify metrics arrived

After running mcollect, confirm the data is in the metrics index:

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`| mcatalog values(metric_name) values(index_name) values(symbol)
    WHERE index=s4c_student_metrics metric_name=stocks.*`}></SplunkSearch>

---

### Step 5: Query with mstats

<SplunkSearch earliest="-10y" latest="now" spl={`| mstats avg(stocks.close) as avg_close
    WHERE index=s4c_student_metrics
    BY index_name span=1y
| timechart span=1y avg(avg_close) by index_name`}></SplunkSearch>

---

### Step 6: Analytics Workspace

1. Go to **Search → Analytics Workspace**
2. Select index `s4c_student_metrics`
3. Browse metrics under the `stocks.*` prefix
4. Add `index_name` as a split-by dimension
5. Compare `stocks.close` across indexes on the same chart

> **Tip:** Try `stocks.volume` with the **Baseline** overlay — volume spikes often coincide with market events (crashes, election results, rate decisions).
